Problem

Why is it so difficult to find consise and clear information about a therapist?

The process for finding a therapist often requires multiple methods of searching, dealing with an overload of information, and takes too much time. It can becomes a deterrant for those seeking mental health services, myself included.

Solution

Help individuals by providing all the information to find a therapist in one place.

BeginWell is a website focused on the first step of finding a local therapist for individuals looking for the first time or starting the process again. By providing all the information in one place, BeginWell provides a process for finding a therapist that is familiar to the user and one that they can trust.

01. Discovery

To begin solving the problem of finding a therapist, I first had to discover if the difficulty of finding a therapist was a problem that exists among young adults, and collect information from potential users. Though it was easy to relate to my personal experiences, I am not every user and I needed to keep an unbiased focus throughout the project.

01.01 / User Survey

I conducted a survey with 33 participants with the goals to discover if young adults are seeking therapists now or will in the future, their current process to access information, their frustrations, if any, in the process, and their decision making process for contacting a therapist.

I realized at this point, I only had an idea what would be important to me as a user. I needed the survey to uncover what potential solutions users needed the most before working on any possible designs.

01.a01

Users go to different places to find information.

54% did not know where to start.

Frustrations ranged from starting the process to tracking down information.

Background + personal statement = trust

For 97%, type of therapy is important.

Trusting a therapist starts with learning more about the therapist and the services they offer.

01.02 / User Personas

With survey results in hand, I conducted follow up interviews with 3 participants to learn more about their experiences. As a result, the personas created representational users that would inform my design decisions later in the process.

Even though I prepared a list of questions, I learned the interviews were more successful as casual conversations. I was able to pull quotes directly from the conversations to use as part of the personas.
Being able to refer back to the personas while making later design decisions was a big takeaway for me. Spending some time getting to know my personas helped create a narrative for each that served as a guiding baseline.
